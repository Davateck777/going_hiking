Patagonia ambassadors clock in on rivers, trails, sick lines and big swells. After work, they change into what river guide Hilary Hutcheson calls her “hard-chilling après clothes.” Here are a few of their durable daily favorites, designed for comfort and freedom of movement while dreaming up your next misadventure.
What Sonnie Trotter Rocks Off-Rock
Men’s Organic Cotton Corduroy Jeans
Men’s ’73 Skyline Organic T-Shirt
“When I’m not climbing, hiking or biking […] I wear the Corduroy Jeans for lunch meetings, grocery shopping and running the kids to after-school programming. They are soft, comfortable and feel natural.

“I like my T-shirts to feel light and relaxed. The ’73 Skyline shirts are soft, breathable and built for everyday living. Plus, they are 100% organic cotton. What’s not to LOVE?”
Moona Whyte’s Post-Shred Threads
Women’s Barely Baggies™ Shorts
Women’s Fjord Flannel Shirt
“Baggies are my go-to shorts for so many activities from working on the farm, hiking or just checking the surf. They are lightweight, durable, and get comfier the more you wear them.

“I love the Fjord Flannel as a quick cover-up pre- and post-surf for a bit of warmth while also being cute. I get a size or two up for an oversized fit.”
Marie-France Roy’s After-Slayer Layers
Women’s Los Gatos 1/4-Zip Fleece
Women’s Ahnya Pullover
“The Los Gatos Fleece is probably my #1 Patagonia lifestyle piece of all time. I wear it so much that people sometimes ask, ‘Do you ever wear anything else?!’ I just love the warmth and coziness of it; it feels like a never-ending hug. Especially useful here on the west coast of Vancouver Island where the climate is always cooler by the ocean.

“The Ahnya is a nice light hoody that you can bring anywhere, from going out to run errands, dog walks, stretching in the morning, etc. I love the timeless cropped style.”
Hilary Hutcheson’s Off-River Quiver
Women’s Garden Island Dress
Women’s Iron Forge® Double Knee Pants
“While I am always comfortable in my river clothes during the work day, I have a ritual where I strip down the second I’m off the river and change into my ‘house dress.’ It’s the Patagonia Garden Island Dress, and I wear it at camp (while guiding multiday trips with clients) or home when I’m done fishing.

“If the weather is cold at camp, I change into my Patagonia Workwear hemp pants. I typically pair them with a Pataloha® shirt at camp or at home to feel festive and chill.”
